Dinosaur Encyclopedia

Velociraptor

Fast and smart!

A small carnivorous dinosaur of the genus Velociraptor , from the late Cretaceous period, capable of leaping, and growing to a length of about 6 feet (2 meters), having feathers, a flat snout, short forelimbs with large handlike talons, and a large sickle-shaped claw on each foot.

T-Rex

Big and scary!

Tyrannosaurus Rex, or T. rex, was a formidable carnivorous dinosaur that roamed North America during the late Cretaceous period, about 68 million years ago

Triceratops

Three horns!

Triceratops was a large, quadrupedal, herbivorous dinosaur known for its distinctive three horns and bony frill. It lived during the late Cretaceous period, roughly 68 to 66 million years ago, in what is now western North America.

Stegosaurus

Spiky back!

Stegosaurus was a large, herbivorous dinosaur known for its distinctive double row of kite-shaped plates along its back and the spikes on its tail, which lived during the late Jurassic period, approximately 155 to 150 million years ago.

Brachiosaurus

Long neck!

Brachiosaurus was a massive, long-necked herbivorous dinosaur that lived during the late Jurassic period, characterized by its long front legs and high-reaching neck, allowing it to feed on tall vegetation.

Spinosaurus

Water hunter!

Spinosaurus was a large, semi-aquatic carnivorous dinosaur known for its distinctive sail-like structure on its back, which lived during the late Cretaceous period, approximately 100 to 93 million years ago.

Diplodocus

Long tail!

Diplodocus was a long-necked, herbivorous dinosaur known for its elongated body and tail, which lived during the late Jurassic period, approximately 154 to 150 million years ago.
